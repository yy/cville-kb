The [[Policy/Charlottesville Development Code]] (Chapter 34, adopted December 18, 2023; amended February 17, 2026) eliminated minimum vehicle parking requirements and introduced minimum bicycle parking requirements (Division 4.5).

## Vehicle parking (Sec. 4.5.4)

**Off-street parking is not required.** Any project with over 50,000 SF of gross floor area must submit a Traffic Demand Management (TDM) plan, prepared by a qualified professional, to be approved by the Administrator.

Where parking is provided:
- **Accessible parking**: per Code of Virginia accessibility requirements
- **EV charging**: parking lots with 6+ spaces must equip 20% of spaces with conduit and electrical capacity for EV charging
- **Reserved parking**: max 1 space per efficiency/1-BR unit, max 2 per 2-BR unit; nonresidential max 1/3 of total spaces

## Bicycle parking (Sec. 4.5.2)

Minimum bicycle parking is required for all uses, except projects with 1–4 dwelling units and units with individually accessed private garages (exempt from long-term).

### Minimum spaces required

| Use | Short-term | Long-term |
|---|---|---|
| Household living | 1 / 10 units, 2 min | 1 / unit |
| Group living | 1 / 6 beds, 2 min | 1 / 3 beds |
| Public, civic, institutional | 1 / 5,000 SF, 2 min | 1 / 10,000 SF, 2 min |
| Commercial | 1 / 2,500 SF, 2 min | 1 / 5,000 SF, 2 min |
| Industrial | 1 / 10,000 SF, 2 min | 1 / 5,000 SF, 2 min |

### Design standards

- Minimum space size: 2.5 ft wide x 6 ft long
- Racks must support the frame at two points and allow frame + one wheel to be locked
- 3 ft clearance from walls, fences, curbs
- Short-term: publicly accessible, within 100 ft of building
- Long-term: covered, secured (locked room, locker, fenced area with locked gate, or monitored area), within 200 ft of building
- Long-term must provide electrical outlets for at least 25% of spaces (relevant for [[E-bikes]] charging)
