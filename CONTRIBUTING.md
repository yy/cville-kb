# Content Policy & Contributing Guidelines

## What belongs here

- Factual information about Charlottesville city governance, processes, and institutions
- Summaries of public records, meeting minutes, and government documents
- Links to primary sources (city websites, public documents, data portals)
- Analysis and commentary on matters of public concern (clearly labeled as such)

## Sourcing and attribution

- **Always link to primary sources.** Don't host copies of PDFs or reports; link to the city-hosted or official version.
- **Summarize in your own words.** Don't copy-paste from news articles, Cvillepedia, or other copyrighted sources.
- **Attribute quotes.** If you quote a public statement, cite who said it, when, and in what context.
- **Government documents.** Meeting minutes, FOIA responses, ordinances, budgets, and resolutions can be freely summarized and quoted.
- **Consultant-produced reports** (traffic studies, engineering assessments, etc.) may be copyrighted even when commissioned by the city. Link to the official copy and summarize findings rather than reproducing large portions.

## Fact vs. opinion

- Present verifiable facts with sources.
- Don't present opinion as fact or make unsubstantiated claims.

## Privacy

- Public officials' votes, public statements, and official actions are appropriate to document.
- For private citizens, note their participation in public processes (e.g., speaking at a council meeting) but don't aggregate personal details like home addresses or employers.

## Formatting

- Pages are Markdown files in an Obsidian vault structure.
- Use `[[wikilinks]]` for internal cross-references.
- Include a YAML frontmatter block with at least `title` and `date`.
