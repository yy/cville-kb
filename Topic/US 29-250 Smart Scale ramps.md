Two [[Topic/Smart Scale]] applications for ramp extensions at the **US 29 / US 250 interchange** near Ivy Road, under consideration in the FY 2026 round:

- **US 29 NB / US 250 EB on-ramp extension** — $13,865,000
- **US 29 SB / US 250 WB off-ramp extension** — $10,664,361

Combined: roughly **$24.5 million** for ramp work at a single interchange.

## MPO Technical Committee, 2026-04-07

Committee chair [[Person/Ben Chambers]] (Charlottesville's transportation planning manager) voted against advancing the projects, arguing *"the projects do not address our needs as the city or as a region in this corridor"* — framing them as a county/VDOT matter where the city should not spend its limited Smart Scale capacity. He pointed to the 5th Street Parkway / 5th Street intersection, site of a recent fatality, as a higher regional priority.

Votes:

- Motion to table both projects: **failed** 4–5 with 3 abstentions
- Recommend the northbound on-ramp: **passed** 5–3 with 3 abstentions
- Southbound off-ramp recommendation: **deferred** to the June meeting

Source: [Charlottesville Community Engagement, 2026-04-21](https://communityengagement.substack.com/p/april-21-2026-charlottesville-reps).

## MPO Policy Board, 2026-04-23

The Policy Board voted to advance the ramp projects. The two City representatives voted no, the two County representatives voted yes, and the VDOT representative broke the tie in favor. The projects now move to City Council, whose support is required; Council withholding endorsement would effectively kill the applications.

## Critiques of the underlying justification

Community transportation advocates raised several objections to the case MPO staff made for the projects:

- **LRTP inclusion is thin.** The MPO staff memo cites inclusion in the 2024 Long Range Transportation Plan, but the LRTP reference is a general pointer to the Ivy Corridor study area; the specific ramp concepts were only published in a 2025 VDOT study. The ramps were not flagged in the LRTP's data-driven region-wide needs assessment, and VDOT's own congestion data does not identify the ramps as congested.
- **Vtrans does not prioritize them.** The 2019 Vtrans statewide mid-term needs assessment rated the ramps **priority 4** (the lowest level with an identified need) and only for *access management* — not congestion or safety. The 2025 Vtrans update drops the ramps from the priority needs list entirely.
- **The Pipeline Study is not a recommendation.** Chambers noted the VDOT Ivy Road Pipeline Study was never finished and issued no final recommendations — its "final report" lists options considered rather than endorsing any. The study's public survey ranked corridor/intersection safety, bicycle safety, and pedestrian safety as the top three concerns; the ramps were not among the field-visit findings or the 2035/2045 no-build scenarios.
- **Marginal Level of Service gains.** The Pipeline Study's 2035 build analysis projects the NB on-ramp improving from LOS C to LOS B and the SB off-ramp from LOS B/C to LOS A/B — modest shifts, given that LOS C is defined in the Highway Capacity Manual as *"stable flow, speeds at or near free flow."*
- **Cost inflation.** The Pipeline Study estimated ~$5.6M for the combined ramp work; the Smart Scale applications total over $24M, a roughly 4× increase with no public documentation of the driver.
