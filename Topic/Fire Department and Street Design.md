Fire departments prioritize equipment access and rapid deployment, which can conflict with safe street design features such as protected bike lanes, curb extensions, and lane narrowing.

- Not Just Bikes, ["How American Fire Departments are Getting People Killed"](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=j2dHFC31VtQ) ([Nebula](https://nebula.tv/videos/notjustbikes-how-american-fire-departments-are-getting-people-killed))

### Richmond

#### Mayo Bridge bike lanes

The Richmond Fire Department opposed physical barriers separating bike lanes from vehicle traffic on the proposed Mayo Bridge redesign. RFD engineer Anthony Jones argued that barriers would prevent cars from pulling into the bike lane buffer to yield to fire trucks, which cross the bridge approximately 14 times daily. This blocked the Urban Design Committee's recommendation for protected bike lanes; the final design uses only painted buffers.

- Source: [Richmonder, "Bike lanes remain a sticking point on Mayo Bridge proposal"](https://www.richmonder.org/weve-heard-clearly-that-it-is-a-priority-for-the-community-bike-lanes-remain-a-sticking-point-on-mayo-bridge-proposal/)
