The [[Downtown Mall]] has two vehicle crossings — at **2nd Street NW** and **4th Street NE** — that interrupt the pedestrian mall to allow cars to cross east-west between the north and south halves of downtown. Both were historically part of the street grid before the Mall's creation in 1976.

## 2026 construction closure

Both crossings are closed to vehicles from **April 1 to June 12, 2026** (~10 weeks) for infrastructure work ahead of the Mall's 50th anniversary:

- Removal and replacement of existing brick and granite pavers
- Concrete repairs
- Storm drain upgrades

Source: [City of Charlottesville news flash](https://www.charlottesville.gov/m/newsflash/home/detail/2497), [29News](https://www.29news.com/2026/03/25/downtown-mall-crossings-close-april-1-construction-ahead-50th-anniversary/), [Cville Right Now](https://cvillerightnow.com/news/208802-downtown-mall-vehicle-crossings-closed-through-june-12/).

### Cost

[[Person/Rory Stolzenberg]] stated on [Bluesky, 2026-03-24](https://bsky.app/profile/rorystolzenberg.bsky.social/post/3mhtnq7feg22c) that the construction cost is **$845,221** (plus design and overhead), and characterized the work as rebuilding crossings "after they were destroyed by vehicles" and reinforcing them "to better withstand vehicles" — arguing the city would save the cost entirely by closing the crossings to vehicles. The $845,221 figure has not yet been independently verified against a primary city procurement or budget document in this KB.

## Pilot closure discussion

In an April 2026 City Council briefing, Councilor [[Person/Michael Payne]] raised the question of closing the crossings permanently, framing the current construction shutdown as an opportunity to pilot the experience: *"I would advocate that at a minimum we at least try piloting it."* He noted that vehicle access across the Mall is a relatively recent design choice, not original to the Mall.

Source: [Information Charlottesville, 2026-04-03](https://infocville.com/2026/04/03/charlottesville-city-council-gets-briefing-on-downtown-mall-action/).
