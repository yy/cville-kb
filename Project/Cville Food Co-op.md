Mixed-use development at 501–507 Cherry Avenue, 0 5th Street SW, and 0 6th Street SW in the Fifeville neighborhood. The site is the former Estes IGA location. Applicant: Woodard Properties. Approximately 1.36 acres with frontage on Cherry Avenue, 5th St SW, and 6th St SW.

The grocery component is planned as the [[Organization/Cville Food Co-op]], a community-owned cooperative grocery store.

- Project website: https://thecvillefoodcoop.com/
- City Council agenda report (September 18, 2023): https://charlottesvilleva.portal.civicclerk.com/event/1666/files/report/1626
- Application: ZM23-0001
- Tax Map Parcels: 290179000, 290178200, 290177000, 290178100, 290178000

## Zoning

Rezoned from Cherry Avenue Mixed Use Corridor (CH) and Residential Small Lot (R-1S) to Commercial (B-3) with proffers. Approved under the old zoning code (prior to the [[Policy/Charlottesville Development Code]]).

SUP granted to increase density from 21 DUA to 87 DUA, with modifications to setbacks and parking.

## Proposed development

- Up to 118 residential units
- ~24,400 SF of commercial space
- Max height: 65 ft / 5 stories (5 ft step-back above 4th story on Cherry Ave frontage; 45 ft / 3 stories within 40 ft of northern boundary)
- Mixed use: no single use type may exceed 80% of gross SF

## Proffers

1. Minimum 60 affordable units if residential portion sold to [[Organization/Piedmont Housing Alliance]]; otherwise 4–5 (up to 8–9) affordable units for minimum 6 years
2. Non-profit space available for purchase for 120 days after first certificate of occupancy
3. Minimum 5,000 SF leased commercial space reserved for a grocery store selling fresh produce until first CO
4. Excluded uses: gas station, auto repair, car wash, crematorium, dry cleaning, drive-through windows, taxi stand, towing service, industrial equipment repair, wholesale
5. Height and step-back limits (see above)
6. Mixed-use requirement (see above)

### Affordable housing targets (Piedmont Housing Alliance)

| AMI level | Share of units |
|---|---|
| 30% AMI | 10% |
| 50% AMI | 50% |
| 60%–80% AMI | 40% |

All units available to Housing Choice Voucher holders.

## Parking (SUP modification)

The project received reduced parking requirements as part of the SUP, below the old zoning code's standard minimums:

| Use | Required parking |
|---|---|
| Multifamily: efficiency, 1-BR, 2-BR | 0.5 spaces / unit |
| Multifamily: 3-BR, 4-BR | 1 space / unit |
| General retail and sales | 2.5 spaces / 1,000 SF |
| Grocery stores and pharmacies | 1 space / 500 SF |

Compact car spaces allowed up to 40% of total.

## Timeline

- June 3, 2023: Community meeting at Living Hope Outreach Church (~40 attendees)
- August 8, 2023: Joint public hearing — Planning Commission and City Council (16 public speakers)
- September 18, 2023: City Council consideration (2nd reading)
- Planning Commission vote: 4-0 to recommend approval

## Community feedback

Key themes from community engagement:
- Support for affordable housing, nonprofit space, and a grocery store returning to the site
- Concerns about building height/massing impact on surrounding single-family homes
- Water and drainage issues on 5th and 6th St SW
- Parking spillover into neighborhood
- Traffic on narrow streets (5th and 6th St SW)
- Pedestrian safety on Cherry Ave (speeding)
- Historic distrust of developers and the City in Fifeville
