The [[Organization/Charlottesville Fire Department]] conducts community-driven strategic planning to gather public input on department priorities.

## Focus areas

- Emergency response readiness
- Firefighter wellness
- Prevention and community risk reduction
- Facilities improvements

## 2026 town hall

A public session was originally scheduled for February 2, 2026 at Carver Recreation Center but was postponed. It was rescheduled and held on March 4, 2026 at CitySpace, with about a dozen attendees.

### Smaller fire trucks (apparatus)

Community members — particularly cyclists and pedestrians — raised concerns about the size of standard fire trucks on city streets. Deputy Fire Chief Will Broscious said the department is exploring smaller "metro design" trucks.

> "Charlottesville is a very bike-friendly community...sometimes when they see our fire trucks, they look massive."
> — Deputy Fire Chief Will Broscious ([Cville Right Now, April 6, 2026](https://cvillerightnow.com/news/208802-charlottesville-looks-to-downsize-its-firetrucks/))

Using Ferrara Fire Apparatus as an example, newer metro-design trucks offer:

- 10 inches shorter than older models
- 2 inches narrower than older models
- 25-foot turning radius (vs. 33 feet previously)

A standard fire truck costs over $1 million with a 3–4 year manufacturing lead time. No specific purchase plans have been announced.
